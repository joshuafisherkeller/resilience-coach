# Resilience Coach visual system

## Purpose

This library is a set of literal picture supports for children ages 6–8 using the product with a trusted grown-up. Every image must communicate its main action, feeling, or body clue before nearby HTML is read. The pictures support the practice loop: notice, name, choose, try, check, and plan.

## Visual voice

- Warm editorial children’s illustration made from soft gouache color, subtle dry-brush grain, and clean paper-cut shapes.
- Rounded, stable silhouettes; simplified anatomy; natural hands; expressive but grounded faces.
- Contemporary, calm, ordinary settings. Never clinical, fantastical, gamified, or mascot-led.
- Backgrounds are sparse and useful: two or three setting cues, a quiet floor or tabletop, and ample warm-paper breathing room.
- Emotion is carried by face, shoulders, hands, stance, gaze, and one concrete context cue—not by symbols alone.

## Palette

Core colors: deep teal `#17685D`, dark teal `#0D4941`, soft mint `#E6F4EF`, warm paper `#FFFEF9`, warm gold `#F4C15D`, ochre `#A57318`, gentle coral `#E88472`, muted violet `#7C6BA7`, and sky blue `#78AFC4`.

Supporting neutrals may include warm brown, charcoal, cream, rain gray, and muted leaf green. Use dark teal or charcoal for the strongest edges and focal contrast. Avoid neon color, glossy gradients, and large areas of pure white.

## Rendering rules

- Use broad gouache fills with restrained paper grain and a few visible brush edges.
- Keep silhouettes clean enough to read at 128 pixels.
- Use soft directional light and shallow environmental detail; no dramatic lighting.
- Faces use small dark eyes, simple brows, softly modeled noses, and natural mouths. Avoid giant eyes, emoji faces, anime styling, or exaggerated tears.
- Hands show the correct number of fingers where visible and have plausible contact with objects.
- No words, letters, numbers, labels, speech bubbles, UI controls, watermarks, signatures, logos, trophies, badges, scores, or confetti.

## Composition system

- Scenario panels: 4:3, eye-level or slightly elevated, medium-wide framing, complete action visible, consistent room and character scale across a sequence.
- Square cards: one action or cue only, centered subject, generous margin, one minimal context prop, readable at 128×128.
- Brand images: relational wide scenes with safe negative space for adjacent HTML, never a fake coach avatar.
- Grown-up images: calm adult nearby at the child’s level, open posture, no looming, grabbing, crowding, commanding, or evaluative cheering.
- Endings show preparedness, choice, or return to ordinary activity. Difficult feelings may remain visible.

## Character continuity

### Sharing and waiting

- Primary child: about seven, medium-brown skin, short black curls, mustard shirt, teal overalls, ochre sneakers.
- Peer: about seven, light skin, wavy auburn hair, sky-blue top, plum trousers.
- Supporting grown-up when present: dark-brown skin, close-cropped hair, cream shirt, muted coral cardigan.
- Environment: warm-paper play area, mint rug, low deep-teal shelf, generic red-and-gold wooden toy vehicle and blocks.

### Making a mistake

- Primary child: about seven, light tan skin, straight dark bob, coral shirt, deep-teal trousers.
- Trusted grown-up: medium-brown skin, dark hair, muted-violet clothing.
- Environment: warm-paper craft table, simple cut-paper collage with one torn or misplaced shape, mint storage cup.

### Change of plans

- Primary child: about seven, dark-brown skin, braids with small gold clips, violet hoodie, ochre trousers.
- Trusted grown-up: light-brown skin, short dark hair, teal cardigan.
- Environment: home or learning-space entry area; rain at a window, outdoor-play items put aside, indoor building or drawing choice nearby.

Across cards and brand images, vary skin tone, hair texture, family presentation, clothing, body shape, and naturally included accessibility supports. Include a seated child or mobility support where it fits the action without turning disability into the subject.

## Emotional calibration

- Frustrated: lowered brows, closed or slightly downturned mouth, firmer hands, mildly lifted shoulders.
- Disappointed: softened gaze, slightly lowered head and shoulders, relaxed rather than collapsed body.
- Worried: raised inner brows, attentive eyes, hands held close, slightly guarded posture.
- Confused: one lifted brow, tilted head, open hand or paused action.
- Calm-ready: steady gaze, balanced shoulders, comfortably open hands; not an oversized smile.
- Unsure: small asymmetrical mouth, tentative posture, gaze moving between two concrete options.

No child appears shamed, punished, abandoned, frightened, or intensely distressed. Warm cheeks, watery eyes, and tension remain subtle and ordinary.

## Literal strategy language

Show only the named action: one comfortable breath; palms gently pressing; hands softly shaking; reaching up and slowly lowering; marching or seated tapping; a quiet pause with an adult nearby; asking for help; asking for a turn; trying one smaller step; choosing the next option; checking the body; or switching strategies. Do not show a magical emotional transformation or guaranteed social outcome.

## Accessibility and safety checks

At 128×128, an adult should identify the intended cue without the filename. Internal sensations may use one soft, non-anatomical overlay—warm rings at the chest, a gentle curved tension band at the tummy—but never medical diagrams. Movement must be comfortable, bounded, and non-competitive. A seated alternative is shown naturally on the movement card. Breathing is comfortable and unforced. All adults remain nearby, attentive, and non-controlling.

## Production targets

Generated masters are preserved in `source`. Rectangular web derivatives use WebP, sRGB, stripped metadata, and consistent crops. Scenario masters target 1200×900 or larger and web panels 800×600. Square masters target at least 1024×1024 and web cards 512×512. Brand dimensions follow the asset brief. Web files should remain under 250 KB when this does not cause visible degradation.
